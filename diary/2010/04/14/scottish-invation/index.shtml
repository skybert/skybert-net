Scottish Invation

For four wonderful days, I had two Scottish friends visiting me in Oslo. It felt like a perfect continuation of my Easter trip to the whiskey drinking country and I truly enjoyed it - hopefully my guests did too!

I tried to show them "my Oslo", so in addition to the usual suspects of the town centre, the palace, the museums, the ferry and the tram, I took them to the forest with Frognesæteren as the spectacular starting point, Mir as one of the most interesting pub in the city, godly experiences in Kraftverket and Blå as the perfect backdrop from the flabbergasting performance of Frank Znort Quartet.

We also ate a lot of traditional Norwegian food such as Norwegian stew, waffles, brown cheese, mackerel with tomato sauce, sour cream porridge and cod caviar.

Luckily, we also got to see a number of my friends and they all seem to hit it off very well. Almost as important was the weather; it was beautifully sunny from the day they came to the day the left. They perhaps now think it's like this all the time in Norway

As I've said before, hanging out with Scots is so great. They're so easy going and of course, my friends were - as friends tend to be - even better than that
