Crammed Packed Week

The last week was nothing short of amazing:

Monday started off with hugely interesting debate about the Norwegian language at Litteraturhuset, after this, I had supper at a friend's place

Tuesday, I taught Norwegian, enjoyed meeting all the people at the Ichtys International Cafe. After the cafe, we went and had a döner kebab before rounding the evening off with a wonderful opera performance in a local pub.

Wednesday, a really good friend of mine from Hamburg visited. We had a lot of catching up to do and made good use of both the evening and following morning before he had to continue his journey.

Thursday, it was a dugnad in my house. Dugnad is a fabulous thing, where people from all the flats in the house join forces and work for the common good of the house. This time around, we painted, washed, sweeped, cleaned and drove away junk. The evening was rounded off with hot dogs and beer in one of my neighbour's beautiful home.

After work on Friday, I go two other friends from Hamburg (think of that, two visits from Hamburg, people I know from completely different contexts, in the same week! What are the odds for that? It was so good to have them, and we did a good mix of sightseeing, eating, drinking and enjoying live music.

Saturday, I went to Halden and took part in the birthday celebration of one of my good, old friends. It was great fun, 13 people cheering away.

I spent the night in Halden and went back to Oslo on Sunday morning. There, I met up with some good friends at a bakery where one of them works. Nice to get an insight into the secrets of the roll making!

After taking a nap, I got myself a nice wee bike ride in the forest. The evening and weekend was rounded off with a night at Blå and the ubiquitious Frank Znort Quartet

All in all, the week was utterly wonderful. At the same time, I'm glad not every week is that busy
