Enjoying the Bright Autumn colours on the West Coast

We went to the West part of Norway for this year's Autumn holiday. First stop was the mountain lodge close to the famous Preikestolen cliff where we spent three days.

I must confess that this was my first trip to Preikestolen and what a trip it was! The cliff is absolutely stunning where it sits above the Lysefjord giving us a breathtaking view for our lunch and plenty of photo opportunities to boot.

The next day, we ventured on a new mountain hike, to the Moslie top. It was a really wet, windy and rather chilly experience as we the rain came down in buckets for most of our ascend to the top. The wind got quite strong too, making it hard to walk straight.

Eventually, the rain stopped, some rays of sunlight filtered through the clouds and we got a nice descend from the mountain and followed a really nice forest-ish trail back down to the cabin.

The Autumn vacation also left room for a couple of days in Stavanger where we got to enjoy the quiet life of this wee city (yes, it's wee), the cosy cobbled stoned streets, low, white painted wooden houses, the charming "Old Stavanger" and its many book shops.

Before visiting some old friends in Stavanger, we also had an extremely pleasant lunch at Bøker og børst, a really charming place with tasty sandwiches, warm and excellent service and they even had an 1978 issue of my favourite comic: Præriebladet (Kaptein Mark)!

All in all it was a great week of beautiful Autumn colours, fantastic mountains, delicious food and good friends.
