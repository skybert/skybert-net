Lijiang

The old town of Lijiang is simply amazing. It’s like riding a time machine back in time. The whole town is on the UNESCO World Culture Heritage list, and is totally impressive. You get perplexed after a bit, because everything is so dream like. You think, this is like Disneyworld And it is, with the important difference that this is for real, the entire town. It’s old, beautiful, situated in the mountains around 2500 metres above sea level, it’s full of craftsmen, making local crafts such as wood carvings and woven clothes. It’s one of the few tourist places I’ve been where the ones selling the crafts are in fact the ones who made them!

I stayed in a beautiful, traditional Naxi guest house called Old Town Garden Resort. The hosts were helpful and trustworthy (often you get the feeling hostels/hotels try to take advantage of you) making my stay there a very memorable one. Highly recommended.

Looking down on my room

Wandering around the streets or just relaxing in the guest house court yard under an umbrella sipping hot tea was wonderful. The weather is also great, quite fresh in the morning, cold in the night time and hot in the day time.

Apart from the old town itself, the Black Dragon Pool Lake is pretty amazing stuff. Mostly, I must admit, due to the fact that it’s the most famous postcard picture place in southern China Be that as it may, it was great to be there and see with my own eyes what I’ve admired in countless traveller’s guides and picture books from China.

Another great experience in Lijiang, was a concert with traditional Naxi (or Nakhi) instruments. Be sure to visit the concert by the Nakhi minority themselves (and not the government sponsored one next to it).
