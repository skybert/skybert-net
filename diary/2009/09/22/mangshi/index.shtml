Mangshi

Once I was there, I wandered around trying to make out where I was one the map. The Chinese are a helpful bunch, but reading maps is not one of their strong sides! I cannot believe that a bus driver cannot make heads or tails of a wee map of his own city (!). Nevertheless, I followed my gut feeling and entered a photographer’s studio, my father being a photographer I thought there should be some global camaraderie - and I was right! The old woman was extremely nice and together using our limited English/Chinese, we settled the matter.

When I finally got a hold of where I was, I met up with one of the sisters who helped me on the unfortunate night at the airport in Xi’an the week before. She and her cousin showed me around in the hospital fist (the cousin had an internship there), we then visited a park showing traditional Yunnan life (straw huts, people in traditional clothes playing traditional instruments), trees and statues with Buddhist annotations, local jade and wooden crafts.

My friends then took me to a Dai restaurant where they served no-nonsense, spicy and hugely tasty food that I’d never had before.

After the dinner, we climbed the hill overlooking the city to visit the beautiful golden temple there. Man there were many steps! After seeing the Buddhist deities and talking to some monks, we climbed down and found a place making local coconut cocktails. Extremely tasty stuff! It was definitely worth getting some allergic reaction afterwards :-)

Before leaving for the airport next morning, we had a great breakfast with local noodle soup, yummy dumplings and banana pancakes. There was also a wee bit time for enjoying some local market with crafts made of bamboo.

Albeit a short visit, I felt that I’d experienced quite a bit of what Mangshi has to offer. All thanks to my friends, of course. I’m definitely coming back.
