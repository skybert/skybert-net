Jinghong

On the way to the airport in Lijiang, I met a great, mad lad from Australia. With such excellent company, the trip to Jinghong was over in no time. The airport in Xishuangbanna greeted me with a spectacular sunset marking a the start of a great stay in this tropical part of China.

We checked into the great, conveniently situated, cheap hostel called “Green Light Youth Hotel” (or “Banna College Hotel” as the guide book says). I paid Y25 a night for a spot in a three man bedroom and don’t have any complaints (if you mind cockroaches crawling on the floor, this is not a hostel for you, though).

After sorting the accommodation out (was a bit challenging as the staff didn’t speak English), we found a interesting/mediocre looking street restaurant. It was a bit slow, but we were starving and couldn’t seem to find where in town people went eating (man, I wish we had tried searching to the south rather than to the north). Of course, they didn’t speak more than three words of English (”fish”, “very” and “good” to be precise). After much ado, we ordered three dishes (got them recommend one chicken, one fish and one cow dish).

Bullocks! Well, not exactly, but what we thought would be a nice beef soup was in fact … sheep’s stomach soup! Yuk. It was at least as disgusting as it sounds like. The stomach “pieces” were spongy and, I admit, I didn’t swallow (or “inhale” in Clinton speech).

The next day, I went biking to the neighbouring villages, including Ganlanba (also referred to as Menghan). It was a great experience following the Mekong downstream, past houses made of straw, rice fields and palm trees. They sold fresh pineapple and I must say; the taste of fresh pineapple is a truly delicious experience. So distinctly different from what I’ve got at home, it was almost creamy! If someone told me it was not pineapple but a related fruit, I’d believe them!

On my bike ride, I met an American couple who had been biking around the world for 11 months (!). Totally amazing story. They were great company and we biked together for a while and shared a meal in the village. Fantastic the people you meet when travelling.

Also on the way, I visited a Dai temple. It was very interesting indeed, looking like a cross between a Norwegian stave church and a Buddhist temple

Jinghong was a really nice experience, it’s so laid back, once you find the good areas with street restaurants and night markets, it’s easy to spend a couple of days here. I’m hugely recommending the Mei Mei Cafe. The staff is so friendly and helpful, serving travellers with good coffee, trip advisory, laundry service, free internet. It’s also a great place to meet people as the rest of the city doesn’t have much for travellers not speaking/reading Chinese
