Doing A Full Circle

I've just come back from a full cirlce around Taiwan. Because of the forecasted typhoon, I had to ditch my plans of biking around the island and embarked instead on rail and bus tour around the island.

First stop was Taipei where I met old friends, visited old favourite food stalls (including my favourite beef noodle soup restaurant, my favourite coffee shop and my favourite beef wrap restaurant).

Since I know Taipei pretty well from living here several months before, I made a number of excursions to the greater Taipei area. It's amazing what you can reach by a train or bus or two!

Also durin my Taipei stay, friends took me to a splendid Jazz restaurant called Brown Sugar which put on a great band with a nothing but spectacular singer!

From Taipei, the journey went onto the charming county of Hualien. After a most delicious lunch, a bus took me up to the majestic mountains of Taroko where I got two days to explore the magnificent gorge, peaks and the hugely impressive Xiangde temple.

Back in Hualien, I met up with more friends, got to try out a number of local specialities, visited a culture park and enjoyed their wonderful beach life at night. All complete with fireworks, flying lamps and (gastly) pubic karaoke where two old sodds believed they where the local incarnation of Wu Bai.

The good old railway took me down the east coast to Taitung and a local bus droppped me off in the vincinity of the guest house with remarkably nice hostesses.

They were absolutely amazing, providing mountaineering shoes (I climbed a wee mountain the next day), their scooter, water bottles, dinner and wine. They even gave us Swiss cheese to the wine! (...one of which they where very happy to give away as they didn't fancy strong cheese).

Exploring the local village of Dulan was a rewarding one. It's very laid back here, things move slowly. This makes for a very relaxing atmosphere and you get to enjoy the old sugar factory, now turned into a culture centre) and some local cusine. Whilst there, be sure to stop by Mama's kitchen. Her local food is really good and the atmosphere is top notch!

Going back from Taitung was as easy as jumping on the train to Taipei (!) which to my surprise went by the way of Kaoshiung and Tainan and the west coast to the capital, instead of going up along the east coast which would by far be the fastest as most logical.

All in all it was a great trip where I got to see many old friends again, visit old favourites in Taipei, explore new places and meet many friendly strangers.
