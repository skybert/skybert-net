Singing the Praise of Taiwanese Hospitals

In my own beloved country Norway and many other countries, the tendency is to centralise hospital services. The idea is that one big hospital with all features and serverices is more cost effective and effecient than several small ones with a basic set of services. However, the down side of this desire to cost cutting the hospitals, is that they're fever of them, often not in the same city (or county!), there's long queues even for the simples consultation, there's more bureaucracy and last but not least, they big hospitals are impersonal, intimidating giant factory buildings.

Therefore, I find it intriguing and hugely intersting that in Taiwan, the opposite is true: here there are a gazillion tiny hopsitals, typically with one doctor, three nurses and an x-ray machine.

They're hugely efficient and easily approachable. Anyone with the standard government health ensurance can pop in to the clinic of choice. They all have their specialities, so you can easily find out which clinic you wish to go to with your say, bone fracture.

My last visit was even faster than my first one. Including new patient registration, waiting, consultation, prescription and delivery of medicine and payment of it all, I was in and out of the hospital in less than 25 minutes. I kid you not.
