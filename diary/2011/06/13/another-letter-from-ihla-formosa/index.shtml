Another Letter from Ihla Formosa

Dear friends from near and far. Here comes an update on my life on the wonderful island of Taiwan.

Food Is a Big Part of Life Here

I've tasted delicious dishes too many to mention or show you in pictures. I've been blessed with good friends that always take me to great places where I can enjoy the local cuisine.

This picture is from a hot pot restaurant where you cook your own food in a hot pot which itself "only" has a delicious soup on which you base your dish. Some hot pot places offer parties to have a divided pot where there's two kinds of soups: red spicey and lemon in our case. Hot pot is perhaps the Taiwanese equivalent of eating shrimps in summer or the all year around taco at home in Norway.

Hallelujah!

I've found a wonderful church in Tainan: a Presbyterian church which I discovered by chance when I was passing through this city in August last year. They are such a wonderful and warm bunch of people that have welcomed me with open arms.

They've offered several times to show me a church that preaches in English, but I'm not interested. I've found my church, I feel at home here and will not change :-)

After service, I've several times been invited to the aboriginal group, where I've enjoyed traditional aboriginal food and song! As you can see on the picture, I'm joining the choir in singing Taiwanese songs!. I don't know exactly what I'm singing though...

Tones, Sounds & Some Strange Looking Characters

The last months have flown by. Mostly because there has been little time left outside of work and studying Mandarin. Every morning, I've gotten up at 05:30 or 06:00, then commuted for one hour, then studied Mandarin for two hours, commuted back one hour, worked 8-10 hours before heading out into the night for a bite to eat. By the time the clock struck ten, I had turned out the lights to be prepared for another fully packed day!

These two pictures are from the class room of the language school. I got two good teachers who squeezes every drop of energy from me with the hope that one day I'll be able to speak this notoriously difficult language!

The map on the wall is also amusing. From home in Europe, I'm used to seeing the world map with Europe in the centre. And as you can see here, the centre of the map is indeed the Middle Kingdom, also known as China :-)

Life in the Big City

I soon felt at home in the big city of Taipei. Albeit it's big, it's a friendly place, very well organised, clean and sorted. Convenient is an adjective that summons a lot of what I think of Taiwan in general and Taipei in particular. Everything is very convenient.

Admittedly, Taipei seemed at first a bit daunting. Taipei is huge, loud, noisy and (at first) it's difficult to decipher all the information that's being thrown at me. However, this soon changed and I came to hugely enjoy the skylines, the parks, jogging along the river in the morning, the street restaurants, the marble paved uptown shopping centres, the breakfast stalls and the wonderful coffee shops. The Taiwanese really have gotten a sophisticated taste for coffee!

Enjoying the Local Cultural Heritage

Even though I work full time, I still get to experience a great deal of the rich culture omnipresent here in Taiwan. Every day, I pass by several beautiful temples that frequently updates their doings according to the moon and the every day life. School exams, funerals and business entrepreneurship are all resembled in temples - if you only know where to look.

In Hsinchu (新竹市), I've enjoyed several sights including Hsinchu Municipal Glass Art Museum, the views from Eighteen Peaks Mountain Park and the delicacies from Chenghuang Temple Night Market

In Taipei, I feel I've been most places at least once (!), but some of my friends took me to The Lin Family Mansion and Garden which was refreshing experience. On the way to the mansion I saw the most amazing subway wall art I've ever encountered, as well as a lively market with very free style shops and offices, often only being a chair and a curtain next to the house wall.

In Tainan, I feel very much at home too but the city's myriad of streets and alleys still manage to confuse my sense of direction at times. It doesn't prevent me from enjoying the cultural offerings of the city, though and I find myself regularly re-visiting the Confucious temple, the view from An Ping Fort and the sea breeze at the An Ping harbour.

I've also started to explore the forests around the city and I'm positively surprised of the wildness of it as visiting some of the biggest nature experiences in Taiwan (including the Taroko National Park and Yangminshan National Park) has given an impression that footpaths are always paved with tiles or tarmac and cliffs and hills protected with neat wooden fences. Luckily, the forests to the north of Tainan haven't been spotted by the tourist industry yet and therefore feels similar to the forests at home - except for completely fauna of course :-)

As I Pour Myself Another Cup of Coffee

This are the steps I have to follow in order to make myself a cup of coffee:

  1. Take water from the tap into a water cleaning mug (it's of a German make so I trust it does its things well!)
  2. Pour water from the mug into the kettle and make the water boil.
  3. The water from the kettle can now be used in the coffee machine.

In the Land of HTC

After about a month after arriving in the land of HTC my dear Desire when into a constant sleep from which she never awoke. The initial hunt for a repair shop which wanted to take the assignment of resurrecting my mobile phone into life again was a long winded one: "Yes, HTC is a Taiwanese brand, but this phone is different since it's bought in Norway". However, after 3-4 repair shops, I finally got the phone on the way to HTC themselves.

And I cannot commend HTC's service enough. It's amazing. They came and picked up the phone on the doorstep. The repair guy called several times to confirm the issues with the phone. It turned out that the problems with the phone was the motherboard. Hence, they replaced this for no extra fee and also replaced one of the phone keys that was a bit worn (but far from functioning). The whole she-bang took less than a week and when I got the hand set back, it was in a nice and friendly white box saying I'm back. A nice touch and a good experience all together. Thank you HTC!

I am a Norwegian Man in Taipei

Celebrating the 17th of May in Taiwan: I bought some food we have on this day in Norway: strawberries, bread, yellow cheese, smoked salmon, Camembert cheese and red wine. My guests also brought delicious fruits and amazing coffee.

After the brilliant 17th of May breakfast, I took the metro out to the amazing National Palace Museum north of the city centre. Since I've been to this wonderful museum before, I concentrated on the temporary exhibition of Marc Chagall's paintings. Apart from a lot of amusing pictures, I found quotes from his memoirs very inspiring.

Letting the Sun Go Down on Me

There are so many spectacular sunsets in this country! I always have my wee camera with me and try to frame some of these wonderful moments of godly art.

From All of Me To All of You

And with these words and pictures, I will now leave you for further adventures on the world wide web and your life where ever you are.
