The Shores of Ilha Formosa

Three weeks have passed since I washed upon the shores of Ilha Formosa. The weeks have been fully packed with wonderful experiences, delicious foods too many to mention, friendly strangers, amazing friends and a whole set of new routines.

To make the gigantic city smaller and more personal, I've created a set of my own routines; I have my favourite morning dumpling (shao long bao) place - that's indeed the same I had when visiting Taipei in August and the woman was thrilled to see me again! - my favourite bakery (I'm already being treated very well with free cakes every day, just a pity I don't eat them, but I don't have the heart to tell the owner), my favourite beef noodle soup (they're so nice, treating me very well, also a plus is that they don't speak English there), favourite evening dinner/snack on the corner (glorious wrapped beef with sweet sauce; nuo rou jien bin), favourite morning jogging route, favourite grocery shop and last, but not least, favourite coffee shop.

I'm extremely fortunate to have so many good friends in Taiwan. They've been fantastic, helping me to get a Taiwanese mobile phone number and a brilliant flat on the 17th floor in the one of the cities many sky scrapers overlooking the river. My friends also helped me to go shopping for house things, as well as furniture, driving and carding everything into my new home. Man, I feel so welcome.

The other day, I also joined the local GNU/Linux and open source scene, attending a hugely interesting meeting on security and anonymity on the net. It's so inspiring to be around so many gifted people. I'm definitely looking forward to attending their hack evenings, dinners and lectures.

Right now, the last, major hurdle is the language. I've gotten a few steps on after my arrival here, but it's still on a "Me Tarzan, you Jane" level and it's at times utterly frustrating. Still, it's at least enough to get food and order drinks (yes, I even manage to get hot coffee and mango milk without a boat load of sugar). I just missed the bus for the university Mandarin course, so I'll sign up for a local one until I can get on board for the next three months period in the university.

I've gotten to appreciate Skype's call to phone services for the first time and Google Talk/Video & Skype are as always indispensable.

Work wise, it's been a smooth ride. Except for the obvious fact that I'm not in the same office as my colleagues back home, the only real thing I miss, is going for lunch with my colleagues at home and stopping by for the odd chat at the coffee machine. Apart from that, a stable internet connection, IRC, MSN, Jabber, Confluence Wiki, Perforce and email makes working from Taiwan a comfortable experience. I start working a bit later to catch some more time together with the Oslo time (they're six ours after me). A great side effect of this is that I'm online pretty much the whole day together with our Bangladesh office. So, after four years of only having around three hours together with my Bangladeshi colleagues, I finally can work with them through out the day!

And with that, my dear friends, I'm sending you my best regards and hope you by now enjoy the warmth of spring.
